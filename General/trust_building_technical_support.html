1. The Foundation of Trust

1.1 Core Principles

## Trust Pillars
- **Reliability**: Consistently following through on commitments
- **Competence**: Demonstrating technical expertise
- **Empathy**: Showing genuine understanding and care
- **Transparency**: Being open and honest in all communications
- **Respect**: Valuing the user's time and perspective

## The Trust Equation
Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation
- **Credibility**: Your words and credentials
- **Reliability**: Your actions over time
- **Intimacy**: Emotional connection and safety
- **Self-Orientation**: Focus on user vs. self

1.2 The Business Impact of Trust

## Benefits of Trust
- 40% higher customer retention rates
- 50% more likely to try new products
- 7x higher customer lifetime value
- 2x more likely to forgive mistakes
- 3x more likely to recommend your company

## The Cost of Distrust
- 78% of customers have ended a relationship due to trust issues
- 62% will share bad experiences with others
- 5-25x more expensive to acquire new customers than retain existing ones

2. Building Trust Through Communication

2.1 Verbal Communication

## Trust-Building Phrases
- "I understand how important this is..."
- "I'll take care of that for you..."
- "You're right to be concerned about..."
- "Let me explain what I'm seeing..."
- "I appreciate your patience while we resolve this..."

## Phrases to Avoid
- "That's not my job..."
- "I don't know..." (without follow-up)
- "You should have..."
- "That's not possible..."
- "Calm down..."

2.2 Written Communication

## Email Best Practices
- Use a clear, specific subject line
- Open with a warm greeting
- Acknowledge the issue
- Outline next steps clearly
- Provide a clear timeline
- Close with your contact information

## Ticket Updates
- Acknowledge receipt promptly
- Set realistic expectations
- Provide regular updates
- Explain technical details clearly
- Confirm resolution

3.2 Managing Expectations

## Setting Clear Expectations
- Be realistic about timelines
- Explain what will happen next
- Provide clear escalation paths
- Under-promise and over-deliver
- Update proactively if things change

## Example Scripts
- "I can have this resolved by [time/date]. Will that work for you?"
- "Here's what you can expect from me..."
- "I'll update you by [time] regardless of progress."
- "If you don't hear from me by [time], please reach out."
